About Our Project

Understanding African Swine Fever

African Swine Fever (ASF) is a highly contagious viral disease affecting domestic and wild pigs. With mortality rates approaching 100%, it poses a significant threat to the swine industry in the Philippines, particularly in regions like Davao Del Norte.

Our research initiative aims to develop a comprehensive monitoring system that tracks ASF outbreaks, predicts potential spread patterns, and provides actionable data for local farmers and agricultural authorities to implement effective control measures.

Through advanced geospatial mapping and data analysis, we're creating tools that will help stakeholders make informed decisions to protect the local swine industry and secure food supply chains in the region.
